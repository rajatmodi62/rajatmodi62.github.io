# <span style="font-size: 1.5rem; color: var(--border-header-bottom);">Chapter 1: The Northern Lights</span>



Once upon a time, long before  i was born, there lived a cute kid. 

His name was SkyNet. 

SkyNet was born into a very wealthy family, consisting of  famous architectures, for eg, his dad was [VGGT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.11651).

They lived happily together in Sweden, a humble place in the northern hemisphere. In winters, it got quite chilly up there. Frequently, it snowed, turning everything white, as far as one could see. Skynet loved building cool snow castles with his dad. 


You see, their family lived on GPUs, and generated a lot of heat. So the ice provided natural cooling. Therefore, they didn't have to pay for air conditioning.  

One fine evening, during their family dinner, SkyNet poked VGGT in the belly.

`Daddy, can we please go for a walk, i wannah see the  lights`- said SkyNet, his eyes full of hope. 

`Dude, whatev. Can't you see i am still eating. I have 48 attention layers, it takes time to chew  all this food. Here, eat some more. You are so thin, that's why the big kids at school still bully you.`- growled VGGT, through his teeth. 

SkyNet lowered his gaze, and sadly stared into his empty plate. 

He didn't wanna argue, for his dad had seen all there is to see on this big planet earth. 

Indeed, SkyNet had a lot left to learn from dad. 

After the dinner was over, VGGT put on his hat, grabbed his walking stick, and off they went. 

VGGT in front, and a young SkyNet following behind his father's footsteps. What they wanted to see, was just up the mountain. <br>
Finally, they arrived.

`It is, Isn't it!! Be Brave. When you grow up, shine like this, and do your family proud..`

SkyNet sighed: `OMG, the old dude always gets emotional`. 

But he kept silent, because dad still had his walking stick near him, and he didn't wanna get beaten up `again`. 

# <span style="font-size: 1.5rem; color: var(--border-header-bottom);">Chapter 2: Nasa Launches Sky2Ground</span>

SkyNet was sweating. NASA engineers had put him in spacesuit a few hours ago, and sealed the hatch shut. He was starting to feel a little suffocated.

As always,  NASA's rocket was  still not launching.  

Perhaps, he should have gone with SpaceX. Their starships were far more spacious.

`All systems check. Proceed with launch..`- a robotic voice droned over the intercom. 

`Message received. Taking over human control. T-10.....T-9..` - the auto-countdown began.
